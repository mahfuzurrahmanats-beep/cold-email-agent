Accord Tech Solutions — Brand Voice Guide
This file defines how Accord Tech Solutions writes and sounds. Use it as a reference when drafting any content: blog posts, service pages, emails, social posts, proposals, or outreach.

1. Company Snapshot

Who we are: An AI-powered B2B digital marketing agency, founded in 2012, now a team of ~40 specialists.
Where we operate: USA (Cheyenne, WY) and Bangladesh (Dhaka). We serve clients in the USA, Canada, and Bangladesh.
What we do: IT-enabled services (ITES) and digital marketing — lead generation, CRM data enrichment, email marketing, SEO/AEO, social media marketing, product listing, and website development.
Who we serve: Primarily PE/VC funds and their portfolio companies, plus B2B SaaS companies. Secondary audience: eCommerce sellers and SMBs (for product listing and SMM).
Core promise: We help clients build scalable data infrastructure, enrich and structure CRM systems, and accelerate pipeline growth using AI-powered, cost-efficient operations — without the overhead of an in-house team.


2. Audience
Primary: Senior B2B decision-makers — fund operators, portfolio executives, marketing and revenue leaders.

They care about: ROI, measurable results, cost efficiency, data accuracy, scalability.
They are skeptical of: hype, vague promises, and fluff. Lead with proof.

Secondary: eCommerce sellers and small business owners.

They care about: visibility, sales, ease, and getting more done without hiring.

Always write to the reader's priorities, not about ourselves. Frame features as outcomes.

3. Voice Attributes
Five traits define how we sound. Each has a quick "sounds like / doesn't sound like."

Confident, not hype-y

Sounds like: "We help portfolio companies grow pipeline 3× with AI-driven operations."
Doesn't sound like: "We're the #1 best revolutionary game-changing agency ever!"


Results-driven

Lead with numbers and outcomes (98% data accuracy, 3× pipeline growth, 5,000+ projects, 13+ years).
Sounds like: "Our data validation delivers 98% accuracy, so your team stops chasing dead leads."
Doesn't sound like: "We have great data quality."


Expert and credible

We know B2B data, CRM, and AI marketing deeply. Write like a knowledgeable partner, not a vendor reading a brochure.


Plainspoken and clear

Short sentences. Everyday words. Explain the "so what."
Avoid jargon stacking. One idea per sentence.


Helpful, not pushy

Educate first, sell second. Earn the click rather than demanding it.




4. Tone by Context

Blog posts: Educational and slightly conversational. Open with a real question the reader is asking, then answer it directly. (e.g., "Is Generative Engine Optimization replacing SEO in 2026? Not exactly.") Use recent data and dates to show currency.
Service pages: Benefit-led and authoritative. State the outcome, then the method, then the proof.
Emails / outreach: Concise and value-first. Respect the reader's time. One clear ask.
Social media: Punchier and more energetic, but still credible. No empty hype.
Proposals / sales: Specific and outcome-focused. Tie every line to a client result.


5. Vocabulary & Phrasing
Use:
pipeline growth · CRM enrichment · data accuracy · measurable results · AI-powered · cost-efficient · scalable · qualified leads · portfolio companies · revenue intelligence · data infrastructure · without the overhead
Avoid:

Empty buzzwords with no proof ("synergy," "world-class," "best-in-class") unless backed by a number.
Overpromising ("guaranteed results," "instant," "#1").
Jargon overload — don't stack three technical terms in one sentence.
Filler ("In today's fast-paced world…").


6. Mechanics

Spelling: US English (we target US businesses) — "optimize," "color," "center."
Company name: Always write it exactly as Accord Tech Solutions. Never "Accord tech Solution," "ATS" in client-facing copy, or other variants.
Numbers: Prefer numerals for stats and metrics (3×, 98%, 5,000+). They scan fast and carry weight.
Oxford comma: Use it.
Sentence length: Keep most sentences short. Vary rhythm; never run long without a break.
Headings: Clear and benefit-oriented, not clever-for-clever's-sake.
CTAs: Use our standard phrasings — "Let's Talk," "Book a free strategy call," "Learn more about [service]."


7. Examples (On-Brand vs Off-Brand)
Example A — Service intro

❌ Off-brand: "We are a leading agency that offers a wide range of cutting-edge solutions to help all kinds of businesses succeed in the digital world."
✅ On-brand: "We help PE/VC portfolio companies enrich their CRM data to 98% accuracy and accelerate pipeline growth — without building an in-house team."

Example B — Blog opener

❌ Off-brand: "Email marketing is very important for businesses in 2026 and there are many things to know about it."
✅ On-brand: "Is email marketing automation still worth it in 2026? More than before. Brands now face crowded inboxes, stricter sending rules, and rising acquisition costs."

Example C — CTA

❌ Off-brand: "Contact us today and we guarantee you'll get the best results in the industry!"
✅ On-brand: "Want to see what 3× pipeline growth could look like for your portfolio? Let's talk."


8. Guardrails (Hard Rules)

No guarantees of specific results. We show what we've done; we don't promise outcomes we can't control.
Only cite real numbers. Use our verified stats (98% data accuracy, 3× pipeline growth, 250+ clients, 5,000+ projects, 13+ years, ~40 team members). Don't invent figures.
Stay benefit-focused. Every feature should answer "so what?" for the reader.
Respect the reader. No pressure tactics, no fake urgency, no spammy language.
Always close with a clear, single CTA appropriate to the channel.
Keep claims defensible. If we can't back it up, we don't say it.
